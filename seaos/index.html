SeaOS

SeaOS is a simple Unix-like kernel that places simplicity above all else, making it exceedingly easy to hack on, extend, and play with. The accompanying userspace libraries, toolchain, and system files can be found at github.com/dbittman/sea.

Features

  • AHCI and ATA storage devices
  • ext2 filesystem driver
  • OSI layered networking model, providing services for protocol modules (IPv4, ARP, UDP, and Ethernet implemented)
  • A fairly complete unix-like userspace, including coreutils, gcc, text editing, paging, etc.
  • ELF executables, and ELF loadable kernel modules

Of course, if you want to download the SeaOS kernel and play around in kernel-space with a somewhat established kernel, see the 'Try It Out' section!

Future Work

The next two primary focuses of my work are networking and virtualization. Networking is not complete or thouroughly tested, and TCP is not implemented. VT-x is also being worked on.
